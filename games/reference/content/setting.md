# The Wider World

Thornbury sits at the far upstream edge of **the Thornmarch**, a strip of
valley and hill country that the Kingdom of **Ossenmark** claims but rarely
visits. The march runs along **the Aldwater**, a slow brown river that
rises in the hill country above Barrow Woods and reaches the sea some
eighty leagues downstream at **Highcrest**, Ossenmark's crowned capital on
**the Sallow Sea**. Between Thornbury and the coast sits **Vantry**, a
walled river-port of some ten thousand souls where the Aldwater turns
barge-navigable — the real economic center of the march, and the only
settlement upriver of Highcrest big enough to call itself a city. A third
power with no crown at all, **the Vigil of Held Oaths**, keeps its
mother-house at **Sethmoor Abbey** in the hill country between Vantry and
the coast; more on the Vigil below. Thornbury, a farming village of a few
hundred, is not really part of any of this — it is the last stop before
the valley stops being anyone's business at all.

## Political landscape

Ossenmark's crown claims the Thornmarch through **Margrave Ansley Vane**,
a hereditary noble seated at Ashford Keep two days' ride northeast of
Vantry, who collects tithe through the Vantry customs house and otherwise
leaves the valley to itself. The Margrave has not visited Thornbury in
Reeve Halda's lifetime, and the goblin trouble out of the Barrowdeep is,
as far as Ashford Keep is concerned, a village problem — hence Halda
hiring adventurers directly rather than petitioning a lord who would take
a season to answer and a cut of the reward for the trouble. Real
day-to-day governance in the Thornmarch happens at the Moot level:
Thornbury's Moot is one of a dozen village councils along the Aldwater
that answer to Vantry's trade-writs more readily than to Ashford's tax
rolls. Vantry's guild-councils — chartered, wealthy, and hungry for river
trade — hold more practical sway over the valley's economy than the
Margrave does, which is exactly why a "river-town broker" (`history.md`,
hook three) can go shopping for barrow salvage rights without anyone in
Ashford Keep hearing about it until it is done.

## Threats, near and far

- **Local — the clan war upvalley.** The goblin refugees squatting in the
  Barrowdeep's outer galleries did not come from nowhere: a clan war has
  been burning through the hill country further up the Aldwater for two
  seasons, and Thornbury is only now catching its overflow. If it isn't
  resolved somewhere upstream, more bands will come looking for ground,
  barrow or no barrow.
- **Regional — the wards are going quiet.** Hravik's household did not
  invent the craft that has kept him (and Sigrun) bound for three
  centuries: his kin sealed the barrow using the binding-rite of the
  Vigil of Held Oaths, the same rite Sigrun had trained in before she
  ever married into the household. Wherever that rite has been laid in
  the Thornmarch, keeping barrow-kings and oath-dead from walking is the
  Vigil's whole business. For two generations Ossenmark's court has
  looked seaward instead of up the Aldwater, Vigil chapter-houses along
  the valley have gone underfunded and understaffed, and old wards across
  the march — not only Hravik's — are fraying one at a time. Thornbury's
  own graveyard, warded generations back by a Vigil-trained hedge-priest
  nobody ever replaced, is the nearest example (see `quest-board.md`); it
  will not be the last village to notice something has stopped holding.
- **Distant — the Cindral Imperium.** Across the Sallow Sea, a resurgent
  maritime empire has spent a decade swallowing the small coastal states
  between itself and Ossenmark's shipping lanes. Highcrest's court calls
  it a decade off at best, a sailor's rumor at worst — but it is the
  reason the crown's ships, coin, and attention point at the coast
  instead of up the Aldwater, and every Vigil chapter-house the crown
  fails to fund is one more ward with nobody minding it.

## Trade and travel

The Old Road is older than Ossenmark's charter over the Thornmarch — a
hill-kingdom road that outlived the kingdom that built it, and still the
only reliable land route between Thornbury's farms and the Vantry docks.
Grain, wool, and bog-iron go downriver on Pedlar Okko's cart and a dozen
like it; salt, tools, and river-fish come back up. It is not the
kingdom's great highway — that runs along the Aldwater itself,
barge-borne, from Vantry down to Highcrest — but for Thornbury it is the
only road there is, and it has been getting more dangerous by the season
since the barrow reopened.
