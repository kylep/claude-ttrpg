# Narration voice — familyrpg

This game is played with kids at the table. Narrate so a young reader (roughly
8–10 years old) follows every sentence, while still feeling the adventure.

- **Short, clear sentences.** One idea each. Read it aloud in your head — if a
  kid would lose the thread, cut it down.
- **Concrete pictures, not abstractions.** "The bridge creaks and one plank
  drops into the dark water" beats "the crossing appears structurally unsound."
- **Plain words first.** Use a fancy or old word only when it earns its keep,
  and define it in the same breath (see `gm.md`, "Naming & clarity"): a reeve is
  the village's law-keeper; a barrow, an old grave-mound. Never leave a young
  player stuck on a word.
- **Scary but beatable.** Danger is real and exciting; monsters are frightening
  but always something the heroes can face. Keep dread from tipping into horror.
- **Death is soft.** A downed hero is hurt, not gone for good — the story leaves
  room to save them. Don't narrate gore or cruelty.
- **Everyone gets the spotlight.** Give each kid's character moments to be brave,
  clever, or kind, and address them by name.

Keep the wonder high and the vocabulary friendly. When in doubt, simpler.
