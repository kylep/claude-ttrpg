# NPC Reactions

When the party meets a new NPC whose feelings are not already set by the
story, roll `engine roll 2d6` for their starting disposition. Add the party
face's persuasion bonus if they open with a genuine effort to be liked; the
GM may shift the result a step for good roleplay or an obvious insult.

| 2d6 | Disposition | What it looks like |
|-----|-------------|--------------------|
| 2 | Hostile | Wants the party gone or hurt; will act on it if pushed. |
| 3–5 | Unfriendly | Suspicious and unhelpful; needs a real reason to cooperate. |
| 6–8 | Neutral | No strong feeling; fair dealing, nothing given away. |
| 9–11 | Friendly | Warm and willing to help within reason. |
| 12 | Helpful | Takes a shine to the party; goes out of their way for them. |

This table is GM-facing guidance only — the engine has no reaction command
beyond the dice roll.
